üöÜ Train Problems

When trains move in opposite directions:

$$ \text{Relative Speed} = \text{Speed}_1 + \text{Speed}_2 $$

When trains move in the same direction:

$$ \text{Relative Speed} = \text{Faster Speed} - \text{Slower Speed} $$

Time taken to cross each other:

$$ \text{Time} = \frac{\text{Sum of lengths of both trains}}{\text{Relative Speed}} $$

Time taken to pass a platform:

$$ \text{Time} = \frac{\text{Length of Train + Length of Platform}}{\text{Speed of Train}} $$

Time taken to pass a pole or man (point object):

$$ \text{Time} = \frac{\text{Length of Train}}{\text{Speed of Train}} $$

Speed conversions:

$$ \text{Speed (m/s)} = \frac{\text{Speed (km/h)} \times 1000}{3600} $$

$$ \text{Speed (km/h)} = \frac{\text{Speed (m/s)} \times 3600}{1000} $$

If a train crosses a platform in \( t_1 \) seconds and a man in \( t_2 \) seconds:

$$ \text{Length of platform} = \text{Speed} \times (t_1 - t_2) $$

üí∞ Profit & Loss

Basic Definitions:

$$ \text{Profit (P)} = \text{Selling Price (SP)} - \text{Cost Price (CP)} $$

$$ \text{Loss (L)} = \text{Cost Price (CP)} - \text{Selling Price (SP)} $$

Profit and Loss Percentage:

$$ \text{Profit \%} = \left( \frac{\text{Profit}}{\text{CP}} \right) \times 100 $$

$$ \text{Loss \%} = \left( \frac{\text{Loss}}{\text{CP}} \right) \times 100 $$

Finding Selling Price (SP):

If profit % is given:

$$ \text{SP} = \text{CP} \left( 1 + \frac{\text{Profit \%}}{100} \right) $$

If loss % is given:

$$ \text{SP} = \text{CP} \left( 1 - \frac{\text{Loss \%}}{100} \right) $$

Finding Cost Price (CP):

$$ \text{CP} = \frac{\text{SP}}{1 + \frac{\text{Profit \%}}{100}} $$

$$ \text{CP} = \frac{\text{SP}}{1 - \frac{\text{Loss \%}}{100}} $$

Mark Price (MP) and Discount:

$$ \text{Discount} = \text{Mark Price (MP)} - \text{Selling Price (SP)} $$

$$ \text{Discount \%} = \left( \frac{\text{Discount}}{\text{MP}} \right) \times 100 $$

Overall Profit or Loss (when multiple articles involved):

$$ \text{Total CP} = \sum \text{Individual CPs}, \quad \text{Total SP} = \sum \text{Individual SPs} $$

$$ \text{Overall Profit or Loss \%} = \left( \frac{\text{Total SP} - \text{Total CP}}{\text{Total CP}} \right) \times 100 $$

‚è≥ Time & Work

Basic Concept:

$$ \text{Work} = \text{Time} \times \text{Rate of Work} $$

$$ \text{Rate of Work} = \frac{1}{\text{Time}} $$

If A can do a work in \( x \) days:

$$ \text{A's 1 day work} = \frac{1}{x} $$

If A can do a work in \( x \) days and B in \( y \) days:

$$ \text{A and B together can do the work in} = \frac{xy}{x + y} \text{ days} $$

If A is \( n \) times as efficient as B:

$$ \text{Time taken by B} = n \times \text{Time taken by A} $$

If A and B can complete a work in \( x \) and \( y \) days respectively, and A leaves after \( m \) days:

Total work = LCM of \( x \) and \( y \)
Work done by A in \( m \) days = \( m \times \frac{1}{x} \)
Remaining work is done by B = \( \frac{\text{Total Work} - \text{A's Work}}{1/y} \)

If the whole work is W units and A's efficiency is E (units/day):

$$ \text{Time} = \frac{W}{E} $$

Work equivalence:

1 man = 1 unit
1 woman = 0.8 units (varies by assumption)
1 child = 0.5 units
