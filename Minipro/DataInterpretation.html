Reading and Interpreting Bar Charts:

Bar charts visually compare values across categories. Use height/length of bars to extract data.

To find the average of values:

$$ \text{Average} = \frac{\text{Sum of all bar values}}{\text{Number of bars}} $$

To find percentage of a particular value:

$$ \text{Percentage} = \left( \frac{\text{Specific Value}}{\text{Total of All Values}} \right) \times 100 $$

To calculate increase or decrease between two bars:

$$ \text{Difference} = \text{Higher Value} - \text{Lower Value} $$

Percentage increase or decrease:

$$ \text{Percentage Change} = \left( \frac{\text{Difference}}{\text{Original Value}} \right) \times 100 $$

Finding ratios between values:

$$ \text{Ratio} = \frac{\text{Value}_1}{\text{Value}_2} $$

Comparing groups of bars:

Add the values of grouped bars and apply above formulas for analysis like averages, ratios, or percentages.

Understanding Pie Charts:

A pie chart represents data as portions of a circle. The whole circle is 360 degrees and represents 100%.

To find the degree for a given category:

$$ \text{Angle} = \left( \frac{\text{Value of Category}}{\text{Total Value}} \right) \times 360^\circ $$

To find percentage for a given category:

$$ \text{Percentage} = \left( \frac{\text{Angle}}{360^\circ} \right) \times 100 $$

To find value from angle:

$$ \text{Value} = \left( \frac{\text{Angle}}{360^\circ} \right) \times \text{Total Value} $$

To compare two sectors:

Use the ratio of their angles or percentages:

$$ \text{Ratio} = \frac{\text{Angle}_1}{\text{Angle}_2} = \frac{\text{Value}_1}{\text{Value}_2} $$

If total value is known and percentage is given:

$$ \text{Value} = \left( \frac{\text{Percentage}}{100} \right) \times \text{Total Value} $$
