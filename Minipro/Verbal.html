Blood Relation:

Blood relation problems involve identifying relationships between individuals based on their family ties. Common rules include:

Family Relations: Understand basic family relations such as:

  • Father (F) = Male parent
  • Mother (M) = Female parent
  • Brother (B) = Male sibling
  • Sister (S) = Female sibling
  • Son (So) = Male child
  • Daughter (D) = Female child
  • Grandfather (GF) = Father of a parent
  • Grandmother (GM) = Mother of a parent
  • Uncle (U) = Brother of a parent
  • Aunt (A) = Sister of a parent

Relational Terms: Understanding common terms used in blood relation problems:

  • Sibling: Brother or Sister
  • Spouse: Husband or Wife
  • In-Laws: Father-in-law, Mother-in-law, Brother-in-law, Sister-in-law
  • Descendant: Children, Grandchildren
  • Ancestor: Parents, Grandparents

Key Relationships: Rules for identifying key relationships:

  • Father's Father: Grandfather (GF)
  • Mother's Father: Grandfather (GF)
  • Father's Sister: Aunt (A)
  • Mother's Sister: Aunt (A)
  • Father's Brother: Uncle (U)
  • Mother's Brother: Uncle (U)
  • Son's Son: Grandson
  • Daughter's Daughter: Granddaughter
  • Husband's Sister: Sister-in-law (SIL)
  • Wife's Brother: Brother-in-law (BIL)

Blood Relation Puzzle Approach: To solve blood relation puzzles, follow these steps:

  • Identify all the given relationships in the question.
  • Draw a family tree if necessary to visualize the relationships.
  • Look for direct relationships (e.g., Father, Mother, Sibling).
  • Then look for indirect relationships (e.g., Nephew, Aunt, Uncle).
  • Use the process of elimination to find the correct relationship between individuals.

Dice Probability:

Dice problems involve understanding the possible outcomes when rolling dice. Common rules include:

Number of Outcomes: A single die has 6 faces, so the total number of outcomes when rolling one die is 6. When rolling two dice, the total number of outcomes is 36 (6 x 6).

Sum of Numbers: The sum is the total of the numbers shown on both dice. For example, if the dice show 3 and 4, the sum is 7.

Probability of a Specific Sum: To find the probability of rolling a specific sum, divide the number of favorable outcomes by the total number of possible outcomes (36 for two dice).

Example: The probability of rolling a sum of 7 is:

$$ P(\text{sum of 7}) = \frac{6}{36} = \frac{1}{6} $$

Favorable Outcomes: The favorable outcomes for each sum can be calculated by counting how many combinations of dice results produce that sum.

Example: The favorable outcomes for a sum of 7 are:

  • (1, 6)
  • (2, 5)
  • (3, 4)
  • (4, 3)
  • (5, 2)
  • (6, 1)

Probability of Rolling Doubles: Rolling doubles means both dice show the same number. The probability of this happening is:

$$ P(\text{doubles}) = \frac{6}{36} = \frac{1}{6} $$

Expected Value: The expected value is the average value you can expect when rolling the dice multiple times. For two dice:

$$ E(\text{sum}) = \frac{7 + 7 + 7 + \cdots}{36} $$

Character Puzzle:

Character Puzzles involve finding relationships between letters, numbers, and positions in the alphabet. Common rules include:

Alphabet Position: Identify the position of a letter in the alphabet. For example:

  • A = 1
  • B = 2
  • C = 3
  • ...and so on until Z = 26.

Alphabetic Shift: Shift a letter forward or backward by a certain number to get another letter. For example:

If you shift 'C' forward by 2, you get 'E' (C ‚Üí D ‚Üí E).

If you shift 'F' backward by 3, you get 'C' (F ‚Üí E ‚Üí D ‚Üí C).

Opposite Letters: The opposite letter can be calculated by finding the letter at an equal distance from the end of the alphabet.

Example: A ‚Üí Z, B ‚Üí Y, C ‚Üí X, D ‚Üí W, and so on.

Reverse Alphabet: A reverse alphabet can be used to understand puzzles where the letters are arranged in reverse order. For example:

  • Z = 1
  • Y = 2
  • X = 3
  • ...and so on until A = 26.

Pattern in Letters: Some puzzles involve identifying a pattern in the sequence of letters. For example:

In the sequence "A, D, G, J, ..." the pattern is that each letter is 3 positions ahead of the previous one. So, the next letter in the series is 'M'.
